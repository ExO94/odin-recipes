Briyani

Description

Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic Indian rice dish typically made with basmati rice, spices, and either chicken, beef, lamb, fish, or vegetables.

Ingrediants

  • Chicken: 1 kg (bone-in, cut into pieces)
  • Yogurt 1 cup
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric Powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1 To taste
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Basmati Rice: 2 cups (soaked in water for 30 minutes)
  • Bay Leaves: 2
  • Cloves: 4-5
  • Cardamom Pods: 4-5
  • Cinnamon Stick: 1 inch
  • Salt: To taste
  • Water: 6-8 cups
  • Oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Ghee: 2 tablespoons
  • Onions: 2 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
  • Green Chilies: 3-4,slit
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Fresh Mint Leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Saffron: A pinch (soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk)
  • Biryani Masala: 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • Fried Onions: 1/2 cup (for garnish)
  • Rose Water or Kewra Water: 1 teaspoon (optional)

Steps

Marinating the Chicken

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • In a large bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, salt, and lemon juice.
  2. Marinate the Chicken:
    • Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight for best results.

Preparing the Rice

  1. Boil the Water:
    • In a large pot, bring 6-8 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaves, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Add the soaked basmati rice to the boiling water and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (the grains should still be slightly firm). Drain the rice and set aside.

Cooking the Chicken

  1. Fry the Onions:
    • In a large pot or deep pan, heat the oil and ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnishing.
  2. Cook the Chicken:
    • To the remaining onions in the pot, add the marinated chicken along with the marinade. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is tender and the oil separates from the gravy. Add the chopped tomatoes and slit green chilies, and cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Add Fresh Herbs:
    • Add the chopped fresh coriander and mint leaves to the chicken mixture and mix well.

Assembling the Biryani

  1. Layer the Biryani:
    • In the same pot with the cooked chicken, spread half of the partially cooked rice evenly over the chicken. Sprinkle half of the saffron milk, half of the fried onions, and a few drops of rose water or kewra water (if using).
    • Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the remaining saffron milk, fried onions, and rose water or kewra water.
  2. Seal and Cook:
    • Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can seal the edges with dough or use a heavy lid to ensure no steam escapes.
    • Cook on low heat (dum) for about 20-25 minutes. Alternatively, you can place a heavy griddle (tawa) under the pot and cook on low heat to prevent burning.
  3. Rest and Serve:
    • Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the rice with a fork to mix the layers.
    • Serve the biryani hot, garnished with more fresh coriander leaves and accompanied by raita, salad, or pickle.

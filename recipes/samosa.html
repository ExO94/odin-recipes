Samosa

Description

Making samosas, the popular Indian snack, involves preparing a savory filling, creating a dough, and then frying the filled pastries.

Ingrediants

  • All-purpose flour: 2 cups
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Carom seeds (optional): 1/2 teaspoon
  • Oil or ghee: 4 tablespoons
  • Water: 1/2 cup (adjust as needed)
  • Potatoes: 3-4 medium, boiled, peeled, and cubed
  • Green peas: 1/2 cup, boiled
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Cumin seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Fennel seeds (optional): 1/2 teaspoon
  • Ginger: 1 inch, finely chopped
  • Green chilies: 1-2, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam masala 1 teaspoon
  • Amchur (dry mango) powder or lemon juice: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: To taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves: Chopped (optional)
  • Oil: For deep frying

Steps

Preparing the Dough

  1. Mix Dry Ingrediants:
    • In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and carom seeds (if using).
  2. Add Oil or Ghee:
    • Add the oil or ghee to the flour mixture. Rub the oil into the flour with your fingers until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Knead the Dough
    • Gradually add water to the flour mixture, a little at a time, and knead into a firm, smooth dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling

  1. Heat Oil:
    • In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and fennel seeds (if using). Let them splutter.
  2. Add Aromatics:
    • Add the chopped ginger and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add Spices:
    • Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, amchur powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Stir well.
  4. Add Vegetables:
    • Add the boiled and cubed potatoes and boiled green peas. Mix everything well and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Season:
    • Add salt to taste and chopped fresh coriander leaves (if using). Mix well and cook for another minute. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.

Assembling the Samosas

  1. Divide the Dough:
    • After resting, divide the dough into equal portions (about 10-12 balls).
  2. Roll the Dough:
    • Roll each ball into a smooth round. Flatten each ball with a rolling pin into a thin oval or circle (about 6-7 inches in diameter). Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.
  3. Shape the Samosa:
    • Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with a little water.
  4. Fill the Cone:
    • Fill the cone with the prepared potato and pea filling. Do not overfill.
  5. Seal the Samosa:
    • Pinch the open edges of the cone together to seal the filling inside. Ensure there are no gaps.

Frying the Samosas

  1. Heat Oil:
    • In a deep frying pan, heat enough oil for deep frying over medium heat.
  2. Fry the Samosas:
    • Carefully add the samosas to the hot oil, a few at a time. Fry until they are golden brown and crisp, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. This should take about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Drain:
    • Remove the fried samosas with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

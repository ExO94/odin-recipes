Beef Wallington

Description

When I think of a Beef Wellington recipe in its basic form, I envision beef tenderloin surrounded by duxelles, then wrapped in puff pastry. Beef Wellington can be made from a large center cut piece of beef tenderloin then sliced to serve.

Ingrediants

  • Beef Fillet: 1.2 kg (2.6 lb) beef fillet (center-cut)
  • Seasoning: Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Mushrooms: 500g (1.1 lb) mixed mushrooms (e.g., cremini, button, or wild mushrooms)
  • Shallots: 2 finely chopped
  • Garlic: 2 cloves finely chopped
  • Thyme: A few sprigs of fresh thyme
  • Prosciutto: 6-8 slices
  • Dijon Mustard: 2 tablespoons
  • Puff Pastry: 500g (1.1 lb) ready-rolled puff pastry
  • Eggs: 2 beaten (for egg wash)
  • Flour: For dusting
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons
  • Pâté: Optional (e.g., foie gras or chicken liver pâté)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Beef Fillet

    • Season the beef fillet generously with salt and pepper.
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over high heat.
    • Sear the beef fillet on all sides until well-browned, about 2-3 minutes per side.
    • Remove the beef from the pan and let it cool. Brush the fillet with Dijon mustard and set aside.

  2. Make the Duxelles

    • Finely chop the mushrooms, shallots, and garlic, or pulse them in a food processor until finely chopped.
    • Heat the butter in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add the shallots, garlic, and thyme, and cook until softened, about 2-3 minutes.
    • Add the mushrooms and cook until the mixture is dry and all the moisture has evaporated, about 10 minutes.
    • Season with salt and pepper, remove from the heat, and let it cool. Discard the thyme sprigs.

  3. Assemble the Beef Wellington

    • Lay out a large piece of cling film (plastic wrap) on a work surface.
    • Lay the prosciutto slices on the cling film, overlapping slightly, to form a rectangle big enough to wrap around the beef fillet.
    • Spread the cooled duxelles evenly over the prosciutto.
    • Place the beef fillet on top of the mushroom mixture.
    • Use the cling film to help wrap the prosciutto and duxelles around the beef fillet tightly. Twist the ends of the cling film to secure and refrigerate for 15-20 minutes.

  4. Wrap in Puff Pastry

    • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
    • Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface to a rectangle large enough to enclose the beef fillet.
    • Remove the cling film from the beef fillet and place it in the center of the puff pastry.
    • Brush the edges of the puff pastry with beaten egg.
    • Fold the pastry over the beef, trimming any excess, and press the edges to seal. Ensure the seam is on the bottom.
    • Place the wrapped beef on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    • Brush the entire surface of the pastry with the remaining beaten egg.

  5. Bake the Beef Wellington

    • Using a sharp knife, score the top of the pastry lightly to create a decorative pattern, being careful not to cut through to the beef.
    • Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes for medium-rare, or longer if desired. The pastry should be golden brown and crisp.
    • Remove from the oven and let rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing.
